BTEC LEVEL 3 EXTENDED DIPLOMA IN SPORT

ALPHA Football Academy offers the BTEC Level 3 Extended Diploma in Sport (Performance and Excellence) qualification in association with world-renowned centre for sporting excellence, Loughborough College.
Establishd in 1909, Loughborough College has established a proud, long-standing reputation for providing excellent levels of high-quality training across UK. In its most recent OFSTED rating Loughborough College received an Outstanding for its apprenticeships, including elite sport apprenticeships.
Alongside its partnership with the Alpha Football Academy, Loughborough College support a number of professional football clubs including Leceister City, Derby County, Birmigham City and Nottingham Forest to name a few. Loughborough College students and alumni have also delivered on the international stage, achieving 4 medals at the Rio Olympics and an incredible 30 medals at the 2018 Commonwealth Games, which would have seen Loughborough College finish 9th in the medal table if they were a country.


COURSE INFORMATION

The BTEC Level 3 Extended Diploma in Sport (Performance and Excellence) is a two-year course, requiring 15 hours of study per week, which are fitted in around the football training. The vocational course explores different ares of employment in the sports sector, and is perfect for those aspiring to a career in sport.
Alpha Football Academy can now offer BTEC course to full-time students who start with us in January of each year, as well as regular September starters.

Course Content

    The course consist of 19 units which explore a variety of different areas, including:

  • Anatomy and Physiology and Physiology of Fitness
  • Sports coaching and Assesing Risk
  • Technical and Tactical Skills
  • Analysis of Sports Performance
  • Fitness testing and Fitness Training and Programming
  • Sports Injuries
  • Sports Psychology
  • Talent Identification

The BTEC educational programme is delivered by college tutors Loughborough College, with the students transported to campus by the Academy and college buses.

Assesment

The course is delivered in a vocational manner, with assessment ranging from written reports, portfolios and posters to verbal assesments, presentations, and practical observations.
During the first-year, students will study the BTEC 90 Credit Diploma, which on succesful completion will enable them to progress onto the BTEC Extended Diploma in year two.

Entry requirements

Students studying a BTEC Extended Diploma programme should normally have achieved 5 GCSE's at Grade C/4 and above, including one of either English and Maths (with the other grade no lower than a D/3), or the equivalent in their own country. Alternatively, students who have studied at Level 2 Diploma/Certificate or a BTEC First Diploma in a related subject should have achieved at least Merit grades.
For those students that do not meet the necessary entry requirements, the Academy may offer the opportunity to study an OCR Level 2 Sports Qualification as an alternative.

Progression opportunities

Upon succesful completion of the BTEC 3 Extended Diploma, students will have the equivalent of three A levels.
