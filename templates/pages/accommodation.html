ACCOMMODATION AT THE ACADEMY

The Academy's main residential houses are the Garden House and the Coach House. We do on occassion use lanladies who have been fully checked by our Welfare Manager and hold the same safety certificates as our full-time residential staff. They are situated within a two-minute walk of the Garden House, where they would still be able to enjoy the facilities offered plus take their meals with other students. Our Landladies would normally have between two and six students at any time, occasionally accommodating players when the Academy is closed.
They are very much family oriented having had families of their own, as well as previously fostering younger children. With multi lingual speakers in French & Spanish as well as English, it is very much a homely atmosphere. This type of accommodation also gives the student a little more independence, which is the main reason we look to house older students. It is a shared bathroom facility rather than the en-suite offeredin the Garden House, however there is access to Wi-Fi, space to study, lounge area and garden to relax in.

STUDENT ACCOMMODATION

About the Academy Boarding House

The Garden House facilities include 20 twin rooms with en-suite facilities, a dining room, communal rooms, games room, study centre, conservatory and nearly an acre of walled gardens. Students are provided with 3 meaals a day courtesy of our in-house kitchen, with all full cuisines catered for. WiFi is provided and fully qualified on-site residential staff will be on hand to ensure that students have all they need to enjoy a safe and friendly stay at the Academy.
The boarding house is staffed 24 hours a day, 7 days a week by our highly experienced and qualified residential team and is situated just a short drive away from the Borderville Sports Complex, the base for all Academy football training and matches, with transport to and from Borderville provided by the Academy.

How to find us in Stamford

The Garden House is a historic building, located next to Burghley Park and a few minutes stroll from the centre of historic Stamford. Stamford itself is a bustling market town set in the heart of rural England, and was known for over a hundred years as "the finest stone town in England". In 2017, the Sunday Times voted Stamford as the second best place to live in the UK. Finding us is very straightforward - we are just off of High Street St. Martin's, which is part of the main road through Stamford. Entry to the car park is from Pinfold Lane, which is off of the A43 Kettering Road.
Stamford train station is only 400 yards from the boarding house, and a main line station at Peterborough is only a ten minutes train journey away. Peterborough links to London, Manchester and Stansted Airport on an hourly basis.

By car

Using the A1 from the south, take the slip road signposted B1081 Stamford and Burghley House (about amile after you pass RAF Wittering on the left). Follow the road down the hill into Stamford. We are the first building on the left as you enter town.
Using the A1 from the north, stay on the A1 until the A43 exit (ignore previous stamford exits). Turn left towards Stamford. In less than a mile, 50 yards before the end of the A43, turn right into Pinfold Lane: the hotel car park is the third gateway on the left (the one with the archway).
Using the A43 from the west, 50 yards before the end of the A43, turn into Pinfold Lane; the hotel car park is the third gateway on the left (the one with the archway).
Using the A16 from the east, once in Stamford, follow the signs for London/A1 South. After crossing the town bridge, continue up the hill on the B1080. We are the last building on the right, just after the A43 junction.
