FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

General Questions

What are high performance Academy?

High performance academies are boarding schools for footballers who have the potential to play professionally .They combineThey combine an intensive professional soccer player training program with a secondary or university education so that students have the opportunity to train at a professional level while they complete their secondary education or work toward earning university degrees.
High performance academies are characterized as academic institutions where talented young footballers can live, train, and study during academic year while receiving personalized, unbiased advice and guidance about their best options for playing professionally.

Why attend the high performance Academy in Stamford, England?

The high performance football academy in England offers professional level football training and has access and connections to coaches and official football clubs in Europe and around the world. And, the academy offers a wide range of educational options for English studentd and international students alike.
Both English ans international students can take advantage of the sports-related vocational courses(BTEC Level 3 course in Sport) offered at the the academy while learning about their options to play soccer professionally in Europe. The soccer academy also offers university courses through loughborough College where students can study a three-year BA Sports Management or a BS in Sports Coaching.

What do students learn in the Academy?

Attending an academy is a unique and very enriching experience. The programme is focused around football where the students learn new skills or improve their technique. However, these programmes go far beyond simply taking a part in sport. The young people learn to be independent, they learn about decision making, discipline and sharing with others, working as a team and a lot of other important values besides. student take part in language study. Student come from a wide range of countries and so the young people have the chance to make friends and practise their language skills with other boys and girls from many different cultures. In summary, attending Alpha Football Academy is an experience which benefits the young person in a much wider sense than simply improving their sporting skills and which will help them to mature and grow as people.

We offer football programme that combine normal academic study with intensive sports training. These programmes are designed for children who want to develop their sporting skills to a high level. The high performance soccer academy in England promotes a flexible international learning enviroment which caters to linguistic and cultural differences. Some international students may need to apply for a student visa to attend the accademy, but applications are usually accepted.
The soccer academy in England offers the perfect opportunity for international students who want to experience life abroad while working hard to reach their maximum potential on the soccer field.

What is a typical day like at the Academy?

From Monday to Friday, the students follow an organized timetable. The day is divided into three primary parts: classes, training, and rest. In some cases, students train in the morning and attend class in the afternoon; in other cases, students follow the opposite schedule with classes in the morning and training in the afternoon.
Weekends are ussually dedicated to matches and/or tournaments and rest. On some weekends,the academies excursions so that students can explore the city where the academy is located and its surrounding areas.
During free time, students have access to the academy's top-of-the-line facilities which include study rooms, game rooms, common rooms, gardens, etc.

What is FIFA International clearance?

FIFA International Clearance applies to any player over the age of 10 who is seeking to register for a club in England and has previously been registered for a club abroad (including the other Home Associations), or who is a foreign national seeking to register for the first time in this country.
If the player is under age of 18, they are subjected to the provisions of FIFA Article 19. International Clearance is applicable at all levels of the game.

Extract of FIFA Article 19 Protection of minors Rule

International transfers of players are only permitted if the player is over the age of 18, unless one of the three exceptions apply:
  1. The player's parents move to the country in which the new club is located for reasons not linked to football.
  2. The transfer takes place within the territory of the EU or EEA and the player is between 16 to 18 years old. If this is the case, the clib must meet FIFA's minimum obligations, which includes in England the club competing in the English Football League (which Alpha Stamford do not).
  3. The player lives no further than 50 km from the national border and the club is no further than 100 km away from the player's home.

Work Permits

 Non-EU citizens who have received International Clearance would still require a work permit visa under Tier 2 and/or Tier 5 to sign a contract with a club in England that compete at Premier League level down to Step 4 of non-league pyramid. A work permit is not required to play for a club from Step 5 of the non-league pyramid and below.
In order to apply for a Governing Body Endorsement from the FA, an applicant club must hold a Sponsor's Licence under Tier 2 and/or Tier 5 of the PBS and thereby be eligible to assign Certificates of Sponsorship. To be eligible to become a Sponsor and assign Certificates of Sponsorship, a club must be a member of the Premier League or The Football League.
The FA PBS criteria is due to be updated 1st may 2017, the FA have stated no major amendments to the regulations which are set to be made.
